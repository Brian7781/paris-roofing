## ⭐ Golden rules — read before you click "submit" anywhere

1. **NAP must be byte-for-byte identical.** `(512) 740-3215` with the parentheses and the space. Never `512-740-3215` on one site and `512.740.3215` on another. Google's local matching algorithm rewards exact matches.
2. **Always pick "Service Area Business" (SAB).** Never publish a street address publicly. Verification addresses (GBP postcard, etc.) can be hidden from the public listing.
3. **Same primary category on every site:** Roofing Contractor.
4. **Same website URL with trailing slash:** `https://brian7781.github.io/paris-roofing/`.
5. **Upload the same cover photo everywhere** (`images/hero.jpg`). Visual consistency reinforces entity recognition for Google's crawlers.
6. **When a platform asks who owns the business, always answer Terry Paris.** When it asks who to contact, you can put Richard. These are two different fields on most pro directories (Angi, Thumbtack, Houzz Pro).
7. **Log every submission in the tracker at the bottom of this file.** The single biggest citation-building failure mode is losing track of which email/password was used where.

## 1. Google Business Profile  ★★★★★

**Direct URL:** <https://business.google.com/create>

- **Cost:** Free
- **Verification:** Postcard (3–14 days) or phone/video (varies by category trust). Roofing contractors often qualify for video verification — prefer this if offered, it's faster.
- **Ranks for:** Google Search, Google Maps, Google Assistant, Android/Nest voice
- **Who signs in:** Use the account-manager email (the fresh `parisroofingservices@gmail.com`). You can add Terry as a co-owner later under *Users → Add* so he also has admin access.
- **Critical setting:** When asked *"Where do you serve your customers?"* pick **"I deliver goods and services to my customers"** → then **"I don't serve customers at my business address"**. This is Google's SAB flow and hides the street address from the public listing while still letting you receive the verification postcard privately.
- **Category field:** paste `Roofing contractor` — accept the Google-suggested auto-complete exactly as shown
- **Description field:** use the **≤750 char** LONG description above
- **Services:** add each bullet from the services list as a separate service entry
- **Service areas:** add each of the 17 cities one at a time (Google requires this city-by-city, no bulk paste)
- **Hours:** Mon–Sat 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM, Sunday closed
- **Attributes to toggle ON (if offered):** `Free estimates`, `Online estimates`, `Emergency service`, `Onsite services`
- **Upload:** cover photo = `hero.jpg`, logo = PR navy mark

**After verification — the single biggest ranking accelerator:** request a Google review from the first 3 satisfied customers on day one. Google treats early reviews with huge weight. Also add 3–5 job photos per week for the first month.

You (Richard) will hold the logins, but Terry needs a way to recover every account if you ever step back from the project. For the Priority 1 platforms specifically, do this:

- **Google Business Profile:** Add Terry as an additional **Owner** (not just Manager) under *Users → Add*. Two owners = bus-factor protection.

- **Yelp:** Yelp allows only one login per business, but you can share it through a password manager (1Password shared vault works well).
- **Apple Business Connect:** assign Terry's Apple ID as a **co-collaborator** under the business's team settings.
- **BBB:** list Terry as the official business owner on the application — this is required anyway, but make sure his name is on the "Owner" line, not just "Contact".

Document the recovery email and password-manager vault name in the same spreadsheet where you track submissions. The goal: if Richard disappears tomorrow, Terry can reclaim every account in under 30 minutes.
